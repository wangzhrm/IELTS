Relative Clauses Quiz (IELTS 7.0 Level)

1. The scientist ______ discovered the theory of relativity is world-famous.

Correct Answer: B. who
Explanation: The relative pronoun refers to "the scientist" (a person) and functions as the subject of the clause. "Who" is used for people as the subject, while "which" is for things, "whose" shows possession, and "that" is less preferred in formal contexts.

2. The book ______ you lent me is incredibly engaging.

Correct Answer: D. / (omit)
Explanation: The relative pronoun refers to "the book" (a thing) and is the object of the clause. In such cases, "which" or "that" can be omitted in informal English. "Who" is for people, and "whose" indicates possession.

3. The city ______ I was born is known for its cultural heritage.

Correct Answer: A. where
Explanation: The relative adverb "where" is used for places and refers to "the city" in this clause, acting as an adverbial. "Which" and "that" are pronouns, not suitable here, and "who" is for people.

4. The only person ______ understood the instructions is very talented.

Correct Answer: D. that
Explanation: When the antecedent is modified by "only," the relative pronoun "that" is preferred. Here, it refers to "person" as the subject. "Who" is also possible but less specific in this context, "which" is for things, and "whose" is for possession.

5. The reason ______ he left the job was unclear.

Correct Answer: A. why
Explanation: The relative adverb "why" is used for reasons and refers to "the reason." "Which" and "that" are pronouns, and omitting the relative word here would make the sentence grammatically incorrect.

Correct Answer: B. whose
