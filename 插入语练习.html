Parenthetical Expressions Practice (IELTS 7.0 Level)

Part 1: Multiple-Choice Questions

1. This strategy, ______, is highly effective.

Correct Answer: A. I believe
Explanation: "I believe" expresses the speaker’s opinion about the strategy’s effectiveness, fitting the context of stating a viewpoint.

2. ______, the event was well-organized.

Correct Answer: A. In my opinion
Explanation: "In my opinion" conveys the speaker’s viewpoint about the event’s organization, suitable for this context.

3. Many animals, ______, tigers and elephants, are protected.

Correct Answer: B. for example
Explanation: "For example" introduces specific instances (tigers and elephants) to illustrate the statement about protected animals.

4. The results, ______, were unexpected.

Correct Answer: C. you know
Explanation: "You know" draws attention to the surprising nature of the results, engaging the listener conversationally.

5. ______, this policy is too restrictive.

Correct Answer: D. I suppose
Explanation: "I suppose" expresses the speaker’s tentative opinion about the policy’s restrictiveness, fitting the context.

6. The plan, ______, requires more resources.

Correct Answer: A. in my opinion
Explanation: "In my opinion" expresses the speaker’s viewpoint about the plan’s resource needs.

7. Some sports, ______, soccer and basketball, are globally popular.

Correct Answer: B. such as
Explanation: "Such as" introduces examples (soccer and basketball) to support the statement about popular sports.

8. ______, the project was completed ahead of schedule.

Correct Answer: A. To everyone’s surprise
Explanation: "To everyone’s surprise" expresses the unexpected nature of the project’s early completion.

9. His decision, ______, caused some controversy.

Part 2: Identify the Parenthetical Expression

41. Identify the parenthetical expression in: "The book, I believe, is a classic."

Correct Answer: I believe
Explanation: "I believe" is the parenthetical expression, expressing the speaker’s opinion and separated by commas.

42. Identify the parenthetical expression in: "Many animals, for example, lions and tigers, are endangered."

Correct Answer: for example
Explanation: "For example" introduces examples and is set off by commas, making it the parenthetical expression.

43. Identify the parenthetical expression in: "The results, you know, were surprising."

Correct Answer: you know
Explanation: "You know" draws attention to the statement and is separated by commas, identifying it as the parenthetical expression.

44. Identify the parenthetical expression in: "Generally speaking, the climate here is mild."

Correct Answer: Generally speaking
Explanation: "Generally speaking" indicates a general observation and is set off by a comma, making it the parenthetical expression.

45. Identify the parenthetical expression in: "Your plan, if I may say so, needs revision."

Correct Answer: if I may say so
Explanation: "If I may say so" politely introduces the speaker’s opinion and is separated by commas.

Part 3: Rewrite Sentences with Parenthetical Expressions

46. Rewrite "This is a good solution." using "in my opinion".

Correct Answer: This is, in my opinion, a good solution.
Explanation: "In my opinion" is inserted to express the speaker’s viewpoint, set off by commas for clarity.

47. Rewrite "The project succeeded." using "to everyone’s surprise".

Correct Answer: The project, to everyone’s surprise, succeeded.
Explanation: "To everyone’s surprise" is added to highlight the unexpected success, separated by commas.

48. Rewrite "She speaks three languages: Spanish, French, and German." using "namely".

Correct Answer: She speaks three languages, namely Spanish, French, and German.
Explanation: "Namely" specifies the languages, set off by a comma to act as a parenthetical expression.

49. Rewrite "The meeting was unproductive." using "frankly speaking".

Correct Answer: Frankly speaking, the meeting was unproductive.
Explanation: "Frankly speaking" introduces the speaker’s honest opinion, placed at the sentence start with a comma.

50. Rewrite "He learned the basics quickly." using "believe it or not".

Correct Answer: Believe it or not, he learned the basics quickly.
Explanation: "Believe it or not" draws attention to the surprising speed of learning, set off by a comma.
