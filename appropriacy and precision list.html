IELTS Writing Vocabulary: Formal vs. Informal

A high score in the IELTS Writing test requires using vocabulary that is both appropriate and precise. This guide provides a list of common informal words and expressions and suggests more formal, academic alternatives.

General Words and Phrases

Informal / Casual Expression Formal / Academic Alternative
a lot of / lots ofnumerous, a significant number of, a substantial amount of, a considerable amount of
getobtain, acquire, receive, implement
thingsissues, factors, elements, aspects, phenomena, problems
have tomust, be required to, it is essential to
so / a bittherefore, consequently, as a result, somewhat, slightly
I thinkIt is believed that, it can be argued that, from my perspective, in my opinion
in the endultimately, finally, in conclusion, as a final point
big / hugesignificant, substantial, immense, extensive, considerable
good / badbeneficial, positive, favorable, detrimental, negative, adverse, unfavorable
very + [adjective]extremely, highly, intensely, deeply, profoundly
go up / increaseincrease, rise, surge, escalate, grow
go down / decreasedecrease, fall, decline, diminish, reduce
showdemonstrate, indicate, reveal, illustrate, highlight

Phrasal Verbs

Phrasal verbs are often too informal for academic writing. Replacing them with single, more formal verbs will improve your score.

Informal Phrasal Verb Formal / Academic Alternative
find outdiscover, ascertain, determine, identify
give upabandon, relinquish, cease, surrender
put up withtolerate, endure, withstand, cope with
bring aboutcause, effect, lead to, result in
look atexamine, analyze, investigate, consider
deal withaddress, handle, manage, tackle, resolve
talk aboutdiscuss, consider, analyze, examine
set upestablish, create, organize, found
take part inparticipate in, engage in, be involved in
point outhighlight, indicate, suggest, state, mention

Idiomatic and Colloquial Expressions

Using idioms or slang is a common mistake that is inappropriate for IELTS.

Informal Idiom / Expression Formal / Academic Alternative
at the end of the dayultimately, in the final analysis
as a matter of factin fact, indeed, actually
get rid ofeliminate, remove, dispose of
on the one hand... on the other handFirstly... Secondly, On the one hand..., it is also true that...
likefor instance, such as, for example
sort of / kind ofsomewhat, to some extent, in a sense

Common Misused Words

Some words are formal but often used incorrectly. This table provides corrections for common mistakes.

Misused Word Correction / More Precise Alternative Explanation
affect (verb)influence, impact, have an effect on"Affect" is a verb, while "effect" is typically a noun.
believeargue, contend, maintain, proposeUse stronger verbs when presenting a claim in a formal essay.
canare able to, have the capacity to, could"Can" is often too simple. "Could" or "may" are often more appropriate.
and so onand so forth, et cetera, among othersThese are more formal alternatives to "and so on".
alsoin addition, furthermore, moreoverUse these words at the start of a sentence or to link ideas.
sincebecause, as"Since" often refers to time; "because" or "as" are for cause and effect.
