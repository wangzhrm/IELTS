Discourse Markers and Connectors

Using discourse markers and connectors effectively helps to create a cohesive and logical flow in your writing, especially in essays and reports. This guide breaks down common markers by their function.

1. Stages in a Response

These phrases help to guide the reader through the structure of your writing, from beginning to end.

Introduction / Opening
  • Firstly,
  • To begin with,
  • Initially,
  • At the beginning,
  • It is clear that… (Commonly used in Task 1)
  • The chart/graph/table shows… (Commonly used in Task 1)
Paragraph Development / Transition
  • Next,
  • Then,
  • After that,
  • In addition,
  • Moreover,
  • Furthermore,
  • Also,
  • Another point is that…
  • On the other hand, (Can also indicate contrast)
  • However, (Can also indicate contrast)
Conclusion / Overview
  • In conclusion,
  • To sum up,
  • Overall,
  • In summary,
  • To conclude,
  • It can be seen that… (Task 1)
  • Overall, the trend shows that… (Task 1)

2. Relationship between Ideas

These connectors are used to show the logical relationship between different sentences or clauses.

Cause and Effect
  • As a result,
  • Therefore,
  • Consequently,
  • Thus,
  • Hence,
  • This led to…
  • This resulted in…
Contrast / Opposite
  • However,
  • On the other hand,
  • In contrast,
  • Whereas…
  • While…
  • Although… / Even though…
  • Yet,
Similarity / Comparison
  • Similarly,
  • Likewise,
  • In the same way,
  • Equally,
Addition / Progression
  • Also,
  • Moreover,
  • In addition,
  • Furthermore,
  • Besides,
  • Another point is…
  • Not only… but also…
Example / Illustration
  • For example,
  • For instance,
  • Such as…
  • In particular,
  • To illustrate,
Emphasis / Highlight
  • Indeed,
  • In fact,
  • Clearly,
  • More importantly,
  • It is important to note that…
