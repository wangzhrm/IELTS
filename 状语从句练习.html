Adverbial Clauses Quiz (IELTS 7.0 Level)

1. ______ he arrives, we will begin the presentation.

Correct Answer: A. As soon as
Explanation: This is a time adverbial clause indicating "one action follows another immediately." "As soon as" means "immediately after," fitting the sentence.

2. I’ll help you ______ you promise to try your best.

Correct Answer: C. as long as
Explanation: This is a conditional adverbial clause meaning "provided that." "As long as" indicates a condition that must be met, suitable here.

3. ______ she was tired, she finished her homework.

Correct Answer: C. Although
Explanation: This is a concessive adverbial clause showing contrast. "Although" means "despite the fact that," fitting the context of doing something despite tiredness.

4. It was ______ a difficult exam ______ I couldn’t finish it.

Correct Answer: A. such...that
Explanation: "Such" modifies the noun phrase "a difficult exam," and "that" introduces a result clause, indicating the outcome of the exam’s difficulty.

5. He runs ______ fast ______ nobody can catch him.

Correct Answer: B. so...that
Explanation: "So" modifies the adverb "fast," and "that" introduces a result clause, showing the consequence of his speed.

6. ______ you study hard, you will pass the exam.

Correct Answer: A. If
Explanation: This is a conditional clause indicating a requirement for passing the exam. "If" introduces a condition, fitting the sentence.

7. She stayed up late ______ she could finish her project.

Correct Answer: B. so that
Explanation: This is a purpose clause showing why she stayed up late. "So that" indicates purpose, suitable here.

8. ______ I was walking home, it started to rain.

Correct Answer: C. As
Explanation: "As" emphasizes two actions happening simultaneously, fitting the context of walking and raining starting together.
