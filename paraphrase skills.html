IELTS Writing Task 1: Paraphrasing Introduction

In IELTS Writing Task 1, you must paraphrase the given task question's introduction to create your own opening sentence. This skill is vital for achieving a good score.

Practice Sentences

  • The chart below shows the number of new homes built per year in a specific area between 2000 and 2010.
  • The line graph compares the percentage of total sales made by three different shoe companies from 2005 to 2015.
  • The pie charts illustrate the proportion of five different types of household waste in one country in 2000 and 2015.
  • The table provides information about student enrolment in three different universities from 2005 to 2010.
  • The bar chart shows the percentage of people in a town who used public transport over a twenty-year period.
  • The graph below shows the amount of coffee consumed in four different countries from 1990 to 2005.
  • The chart shows the different reasons why people travel to work in a particular city.
  • The diagram below illustrates the process of making electricity from waste materials.
  • The map shows the changes to a park area between 2000 and the present day.
  • The table shows the changes in the number of people who visited three museums between 1999 and 2009.

Methods for Paraphrasing

When paraphrasing, you should focus on replacing key components of the sentence without changing its meaning. This can be done by varying the following three parts:

  • 1. Noun and Verb Combination: This is the most crucial part. Replace the specific noun (e.g., chart, graph) and a verb (e.g., shows, illustrates).
    • Original: The chart below shows...
    • Paraphrased: The bar chart presents...
    • Original: The line graph compares...
    • Paraphrased: The line graph provides a comparison of...
  • 2. Nouns and Noun Phrases: Replace specific nouns and phrases with synonyms. For example, "new homes" can become "residential properties." "Student enrolment" can be replaced with "the number of students."
  • 3. Time and Location: Rephrase the time period or location.
    • Original: ...between 2000 and 2010.
    • Paraphrased: ...over a 10-year period from 2000 to 2010.

List of Alternative Words & Expressions

Here are some alternatives you can use to paraphrase key sections of the IELTS Writing Task 1 introduction.

Category Alternative Words & Phrases
For the Noun (the type of visual): The provided chart/graph, the given diagram, the visuals, the bar chart, the line graph, the pie charts, the table, the diagram, the map.
For the Verb (what the visual does):

Shows/Illustrates: displays, presents, depicts, highlights, outlines, gives information about, provides data on, details, describes, indicates.

Compares: provides a comparison of, contrasts, shows the difference between.

Shows Changes: illustrates trends in, shows the fluctuations in, tracks the changes in.
For Common Noun Phrases:

People: individuals, residents, citizens, the population.

Percentage/Proportion: the proportion of, the rate of, the figure for, the share of.

Number/Amount: the quantity of, the figure for, the number of.

Changes/Fluctuations: modifications, developments, shifts, transformations.
