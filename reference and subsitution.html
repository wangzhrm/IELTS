Using Reference and Substitution for a Higher IELTS Score

For IELTS Writing Task 1, showing a flexible use of reference and substitution is key to achieving a high score for Coherence and Cohesion. These techniques help you avoid repetition and make your writing flow naturally.

What Are They?

Reference is using words like pronouns (it, they) or demonstratives (this, that) to refer back to a noun or idea already mentioned. It creates a seamless link between sentences.

Substitution is replacing a word or phrase with a different, but related, word or phrase to avoid repeating the same language. This often involves using synonyms or more general terms.

Good Examples of Use

Example 1 (Reference)

The number of international tourists visiting the city increased steadily. This was a result of a new marketing campaign.
'This' refers back to the increase in tourists, linking the two sentences smoothly.

Example 2 (Substitution)

The chart shows a significant rise in car ownership. This upward trend was particularly noticeable in rural areas.
'This upward trend' replaces the phrase 'a significant rise', adding variety to the language.

Example 3 (Reference and Substitution)

The proportion of the population over 65 years old grew significantly over the period. This demographic is projected to continue expanding, and it will have a major impact on healthcare spending.
'This demographic' substitutes 'the proportion of the population over 65 years old', while 'it' is a pronoun that refers back to this same group.

Bad Examples (Where Techniques Are Absent)

These examples show how a lack of reference and substitution leads to repetitive and clunky writing, which can lower your score.

Example 1 (Repetitive Noun)

The line graph shows the percentage of households that owned a computer. The percentage reveals a steady growth from 1995 to 2005.
The word 'percentage' is repeated, making the sentence feel a bit clunky.

Example 2 (Unnecessary Repetition)

The total number of students enrolled at the university declined. The decline was due to budget cuts.
Repeating the word 'decline' is unnecessary.

Example 3 (Lack of Connection)

Energy consumption saw a major increase in the final quarter. The new factory was built in the region.
Without a linking word, the connection between the two sentences is unclear.

Tips for Using Them Effectively

  • Use a variety of words. Don't always rely on 'it' or 'they'.
  • Think of synonyms for key words in the task.
  • Read your sentences aloud to check if they flow naturally.
  • Practice paraphrasing phrases from the task question.
